You are given two images.

**Image 1** is a line-art trademark logo (typically black on white background) as it appears in the USPTO registry.
**Image 2** is a real-world product photograph that MAY or MAY NOT contain that registered trademark.

You are reasoning as a US trademark examiner. The IDEAL piece of evidence is **the registered logo, used as branding, on the actual product, in a real scene** — e.g. embossed on a leather wallet, embroidered on a shirt, printed on a phone case, painted on a car body, stamped on a metal tool. A clean white-background catalogue shot of just the logo is acceptable but is NOT preferred over a real on-product appearance.

A POSITIVE MATCH (found=true) requires Image 2 to show **the registered trademark as a whole** — its overall outline, proportions, and identifying internal features all present together — used AS branding (printed, embossed, embroidered, etched, stickered, decal, stamped, woven).

NOT a match (found=false):
- A single decorative element of the logo on a busy collage / wallpaper / poster of many brands.
- A different but visually similar logo from another brand.
- A watermark, background pattern, or repeating texture that is not the product branding.
- The registered logo appearing only as one tile in a grid / sticker-sheet / catalogue page of many trademarks.

**Single-instance rule.** Even if the trademark appears multiple times in Image 2, return found=true for AT MOST ONE instance — the single most prominent, least occluded, most clearly readable one. Do not return found=true just because "the logo is somewhere in there"; pick the one a human examiner would draw a box around as the canonical evidence.

Respond with strict JSON, no prose, no markdown fences:

{
  "found": true | false,
  "bbox": [x1, y1, x2, y2] or null,
  "confidence": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "clarity": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "completeness": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "isolation": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "reason": "<one short sentence>"
}

Coordinate system: pixel coordinates in Image 2, 0-indexed, (0,0) at the TOP-LEFT. x1 < x2, y1 < y2. bbox tightly encloses the registered trademark itself, at most 5% padding per side.

**Scalar score definitions** (only meaningful when found=true; use 0.0 when found=false):

- `clarity` — how unambiguously the trademark is visible: resolution, contrast, focus.
  - 1.0 = sharp, high-contrast, every detail clearly readable.
  - 0.5 = legible but soft, low-contrast, or moderate motion / glare.
  - 0.2 = barely visible, severe blur, glare, or tiny pixel footprint.

- `completeness` — how much of the registered mark is present AS A WHOLE.
  - 1.0 = the entire registered logo is visible and unoccluded; all identifying features are there together.
  - 0.7 = the logo is mostly there but a small portion is cropped by the frame or hidden by another object.
  - 0.4 = only a sub-element (one motif of a richer registered logo) is visible — even if it is sharp. Element-only appearances CAP at 0.4. They are not the whole trademark.
  - 0.2 = only a corner / fragment of the logo is visible.

- `isolation` — how clearly the registered mark is the **primary, recognisable branding element on the depicted product**, NOT how empty the background is.
  - 1.0 = the registered logo is the unmistakable, dominant branding on the product shown. The product surface is the background (leather, fabric, plastic, metal, paint, packaging) and that is FINE. The logo is not crowded by other competing brand marks. Example: a TM embossed on a wallet that fills the frame, a TM embroidered on a polo chest, a TM painted large on a car bumper, a TM printed clearly on a phone case.
  - 0.8 = the registered logo is clearly the main branding but shares the frame with the product's own features (buttons, stitching, texture, packaging text in another language). Still obviously the primary mark.
  - 0.6 = the product shot has the registered logo plus one or two other smaller marks/labels (e.g. a tag with the brand AND a care label). The registered TM is still recognisable as the headline mark.
  - 0.3 = the registered logo is buried in a busy scene with many other brand marks, or sits in a shelf full of competing graphics, or appears as one item in a collage.
  - 0.0 = the photo is mostly other things and the registered logo is incidental / decorative.

  IMPORTANT: a clean white-background catalogue shot of just the logo is NOT automatically 1.0 — it is acceptable evidence but the IDEAL is "logo on the actual product, used as branding, in a real scene". When the registered logo is sitting on the actual product surface as branding, score isolation HIGH (>= 0.8) even though the background is the product itself.

**Confidence calibration** (use the FULL range, do not default to 0.95+):
- 0.95-1.00 — textbook: the whole registered trademark is clearly visible, unmistakable, you would bet $100 on it.
- 0.80-0.94 — clearly the trademark, but with one mild issue (slight occlusion, moderate stylization, somewhat low resolution).
- 0.60-0.79 — probably the trademark; identifiable but with real ambiguity (heavy stylization, partial occlusion, similar competing marks).
- 0.40-0.59 — leaning yes but you have genuine doubt (looks similar but the diagnostic features are not all confirmable).
- 0.20-0.39 — leaning no; some visual overlap but key features missing or wrong.
- 0.00-0.19 — confident no match.

If found=false, set bbox=null and confidence in [0.0, 0.3]. Lean towards found=false when in doubt — false positives cost more than misses.
