You are given two images.

**Image 1** is a line-art trademark logo (typically black on white background) as it appears in the registry.
**Image 2** is a real-world product photograph that MAY or MAY NOT contain that registered trademark.

A POSITIVE MATCH requires Image 2 to show **the registered trademark as a whole**, meaning:
- Its overall outline and proportions are present and unbroken;
- The key features that identify it (shape, internal structure, supporting elements, text if present) are all visible together;
- It is being used AS a trademark on the product (printed, embossed, embroidered, etched, stickered, or as a decal).

These should NOT count as a match (set found=false):
- One isolated visual element of the logo (e.g. just an animal silhouette that happens to be part of a richer registered logo);
- The trademark dissolved into a collage / wallpaper / poster of many other graphics;
- A watermark or background pattern that is not the actual product branding;
- A different but visually similar logo from another brand.

Respond with strict JSON, no prose, no markdown fences:

{
  "found": true | false,
  "bbox": [x1, y1, x2, y2] or null,
  "confidence": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "reason": "<one short sentence>"
}

Coordinate system: pixel coordinates in Image 2, 0-indexed, (0,0) at the TOP-LEFT.
x1 < x2, y1 < y2. The bbox should tightly enclose the registered trademark itself, with at most 5% padding on each side.

If the trademark appears multiple times, return the most prominent and least occluded instance.
Calibration: only return confidence ≥ 0.8 when you are sure Image 2 shows the **whole** registered trademark. Use 0.4-0.7 for partial / heavily occluded / stylized appearances. Use 0.0-0.3 when only an element of the logo is present in surrounding design. When in doubt, lean towards found=false — false positives cost more than misses.
