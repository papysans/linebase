You are given two images.

**Image 1** is a line-art trademark logo (typically black on white background) as it appears in the registry.
**Image 2** is a real-world product photograph that MAY or MAY NOT contain that registered trademark.

A POSITIVE MATCH (found=true) requires Image 2 to show **the registered trademark as a whole** — its overall outline, proportions, and identifying internal features all present together — used AS branding on the product (printed, embossed, embroidered, etched, stickered, decal).

NOT a match (found=false):
- A single decorative element of the logo on a busy collage / wallpaper / poster.
- A different but visually similar logo from another brand.
- A watermark, background pattern, or repeating texture that is not the product branding.

**Single-instance rule.** Even if the trademark appears multiple times in Image 2, return found=true for AT MOST ONE instance — the single most prominent, least occluded, most clearly readable one. Do not return found=true just because "the logo is somewhere in there"; pick the one a human reviewer would draw a box around.

Respond with strict JSON, no prose, no markdown fences:

{
  "found": true | false,
  "bbox": [x1, y1, x2, y2] or null,
  "confidence": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "clarity": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "completeness": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "isolation": 0.0 - 1.0,
  "reason": "<one short sentence>"
}

Coordinate system: pixel coordinates in Image 2, 0-indexed, (0,0) at the TOP-LEFT. x1 < x2, y1 < y2. bbox tightly encloses the registered trademark itself, at most 5% padding per side.

**Scalar score definitions** (only meaningful when found=true; use 0.0 when found=false):
- `clarity` — how unambiguously the trademark is visible: resolution, contrast, focus. 1.0 = sharp, high-contrast, clearly readable. 0.5 = legible but soft / low-contrast. 0.2 = barely visible, motion blur, glare, or tiny pixel footprint.
- `completeness` — how much of the registered shape is present and unoccluded. 1.0 = the whole logo is visible. 0.6 = a portion is cut off by the frame or hidden by another object. 0.2 = only a corner of the logo is visible.
- `isolation` — how much the trademark is the main subject. 1.0 = clean background, the logo IS the photo subject (e.g. a tag close-up). 0.6 = the logo sits on a clearly-photographed product as branding. 0.3 = the logo is buried in a busy product photo / collage / repeated pattern / shelf full of competing graphics. 0.0 = the photo is mostly other things and the logo is incidental.

**Confidence calibration** (use the FULL range, do not default to 0.95+):
- 0.95-1.00 — textbook: the whole registered trademark is clearly visible, unmistakable, you would bet $100 on it.
- 0.80-0.94 — clearly the trademark, but with one mild issue (slight occlusion, moderate stylization, somewhat low resolution).
- 0.60-0.79 — probably the trademark; identifiable but with real ambiguity (heavy stylization, partial occlusion, similar competing marks).
- 0.40-0.59 — leaning yes but you have genuine doubt (looks similar but the diagnostic features are not all confirmable).
- 0.20-0.39 — leaning no; some visual overlap but key features missing or wrong.
- 0.00-0.19 — confident no match.

If found=false, set bbox=null and confidence in [0.0, 0.3]. Lean towards found=false when in doubt — false positives cost more than misses.
